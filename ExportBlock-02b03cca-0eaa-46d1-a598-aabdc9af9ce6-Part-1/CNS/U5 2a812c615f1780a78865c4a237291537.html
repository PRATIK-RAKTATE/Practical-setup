5. Email System

Email services use a client‚Äìserver architecture and multiple protocols for sending and receiving messages.

a) SMTP (Simple Mail Transfer Protocol)

6. FTP (File Transfer Protocol)

  • Operations:

  • Port: 23.
  • Limitation: Sends data (including passwords) in plain text ‚Üí insecure.
  • Replaced by: SSH (Secure Shell).

  • Process:

  • Advantage: Reduces manual configuration, avoids IP conflicts.

9. SNMP (Simple Network Management Protocol)

  • Used for: Monitoring and managing network devices (routers, switches, etc.).
  • Port: 161 (UDP).
  • Components:
    • Manager: Collects information.
    • Agent: Installed on devices to send data.
    • MIB (Management Information Base): Database of device parameters.
  • Example: Check router performance, traffic, uptime.
